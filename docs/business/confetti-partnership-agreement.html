Venue Partner Contract · Heritage Power Group LLC
This Advertising Partnership Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of _______________ (the "Effective Date") by and between Heritage Power Group LLC, a District of Columbia limited liability company, doing business as Confetti ("Company"), and the venue partner identified in the signature block below ("Partner"). Company and Partner are each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties."
01
Definitions

"Platform" means the Confetti application, website (confettiplan.com and all subdomains), and related services operated by Company.

"Venue Card" means the digital listing created for Partner on the Platform, including name, photos, description, hours, and booking information.

"Itinerary" means an AI-generated outing plan created by the Platform for end users, consisting of multiple venue stops with timing and routing.

"Boost Credit" means a unit of promotional currency that temporarily elevates Partner's placement in Itineraries for a specified date or event.

"Advertising Tier" means the service level selected by Partner, as specified in Section 2.

02

Partner shall select one of the following Advertising Tiers. The selected tier determines the scope of services, placement priority, and monthly Boost Credit allocation.

03
Billing Frequency

Partner shall select a billing frequency. Annual and quarterly billing include discounted rates as indicated.

Monthly — Standard rate, billed on the 1st of each month
Quarterly — 10% discount, billed every 3 months in advance
Annual — 20% discount, billed annually in advance
04
Term and Renewal

4.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for the billing period selected in Section 3 (the "Initial Term").

4.2 Renewal. This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods equal to the Initial Term unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least fifteen (15) days before the end of the then-current term.

4.3 Early Termination. Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time with thirty (30) days' written notice. Monthly subscribers may cancel at any time effective at the end of the current billing period.

05
Payment Terms

5.1 Fees. Partner agrees to pay the fees associated with the selected Advertising Tier and billing frequency as set forth in Sections 2 and 3.

5.2 Payment Method. All payments shall be made via credit card, ACH transfer, or other electronic payment method accepted by Company. Partner shall provide valid payment credentials at the time of execution.

5.3 Late Payments. Invoices not paid within ten (10) days of the due date shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.

5.4 Taxes. All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. Partner is responsible for all sales, use, and similar taxes arising from this Agreement.

5.5 Boost Credits. Additional Boost Credits purchased beyond the monthly allocation are non-refundable but do not expire. Unused included monthly credits roll over for up to ninety (90) days.

06
Company Obligations

6.1 Company shall create and maintain Partner's Venue Card on the Platform in accordance with the selected Advertising Tier.

6.2 Company shall include Partner's venue in the Platform's AI recommendation engine with placement priority consistent with the selected tier.

6.3 Company shall provide Partner with access to a performance dashboard showing impressions, click-throughs, and estimated bookings (Featured and Premier tiers).

6.4 Company shall allocate the monthly Boost Credits specified in the selected tier and honor any additional purchased credits.

6.5 Company reserves the right to adjust its recommendation algorithm at any time, provided that tier-based placement hierarchies are maintained.

07
Partner Obligations

7.1 Partner shall provide accurate and current venue information, including hours of operation, contact details, photos, and menu or service descriptions.

7.2 Partner shall promptly notify Company of any material changes to venue information, temporary closures, or events that affect availability.

7.3 Partner grants Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use Partner's name, logo, photos, and descriptions on the Platform and in marketing materials for the duration of this Agreement.

7.4 Partner shall honor all reservations and referrals made through the Platform and provide a standard level of service to Confetti users.

08
Intellectual Property

8.1 Each Party retains all rights to its own intellectual property. Nothing in this Agreement transfers ownership of any intellectual property between the Parties.

8.2 Company retains all rights to the Platform, including its recommendation algorithms, user interface, and branding.

8.3 Upon termination, Company shall remove Partner's Venue Card and cease use of Partner's intellectual property within thirty (30) days.

09
Confidentiality

Each Party agrees to maintain the confidentiality of any proprietary information received from the other Party during the term of this Agreement, including but not limited to business strategies, pricing, customer data, and performance metrics. This obligation survives termination for a period of two (2) years.

10
Limitation of Liability

10.1 IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF THIS AGREEMENT.

10.2 Company's total cumulative liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by Partner in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim.

10.3 Company does not guarantee any specific number of impressions, bookings, or revenue outcomes. All performance projections are estimates only.

11
Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

11.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the District of Columbia, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

11.2 Dispute Resolution. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall first be submitted to good faith mediation. If mediation fails to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) days, either Party may pursue litigation in the courts of the District of Columbia.

11.3 Venue. The Parties agree to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Superior Court of the District of Columbia and the United States District Court for the District of Columbia.

12
General Provisions

12.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements.

12.2 Amendments. This Agreement may only be modified by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

12.3 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

12.4 Waiver. No failure to exercise any right shall constitute a waiver of such right.

12.5 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered via email or certified mail to the addresses specified by each Party.

12.6 Assignment. Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except in connection with a merger or acquisition.

12.7 Independent Contractors. The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates an agency, partnership, or employment relationship.
